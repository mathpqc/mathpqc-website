MathPQC 2026(Mathematics for Post-Quantum Cryptography)

MathPQC 2026 is a one-day affiliated workshop of ASIACRYPT 2026.

Date: Monday, December 7, 2026

Venue: Hong Kong Polytechnic University, Hong Kong

About the workshop

This event is intended to bring together mathematicians and cryptographers who are interested in the mathematical foundations of post-quantum cryptography. The workshop will highlight recent progress in the field, discuss open problems, and foster new collaborations between the mathematics and cryptography communities.

Topics of interest include lattice-based cryptography, isogeny-based cryptography, code-based cryptography, multivariate cryptography, and related mathematical tools from number theory, algebra, algebraic geometry, and the geometry of numbers.

Event summary

Post-quantum schemes rely on several families of computationally hard problems, including lattice problems (e.g. the shortest vector problem on Euclidean and ideal lattices, and the lattice isomorphism problem on Euclidean and module lattices), finding isogenies between supersingular elliptic curves (and superspecial abelian varieties) over finite fields, decoding problems for linear codes, and solving systems of multivariate quadratic (MQ) equations over finite fields. These problems draw on a broad range of mathematical disciplines, including number theory, algebra, the geometry of numbers, algebraic geometry, and many others. In light of these connections, this affiliated event aims to bring together mathematicians working in these fields and cryptographers interested in the mathematical foundations of post-quantum cryptography. The event will provide an opportunity to exchange ideas across disciplines, highlight recent developments, and foster new interactions between the mathematics and cryptography communities.
